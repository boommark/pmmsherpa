## 1. What Smart Brevity gave us (the editorial spine)

Smart Brevity is a brevity / clarity book applied to executive communication. We adopt its full structural backbone for the joint solution brief because the asset is read async by a buyer who did not opt in, on a single sheet, often forwarded to their boss. Every Smart Brevity rule applies double — there are now two vendors fighting for the same word count.

- **Core 4 mapped to the joint brief.**
  - **TEASE** — the joint headline. Stake is the joint outcome neither vendor delivers alone, named in buyer language. Not "Acme + Beta partnership", not "Acme and Beta together deliver…".
  - **LEDE** — the joint Power Statement. One sentence that names the shared customer, the joint outcome, and the structural reason no single vendor can deliver it. The boss-repeat test applies.
  - **WHY IT MATTERS** — exactly three joint customer outcomes (verb + result), not three product features and not three product features × two vendors.
  - **GO DEEPER** — How It Works (3 max steps showing the integration / division of labor) + ONE joint customer proof.
- **Audience-First applied jointly.** The buyer is the audience, not the partnership team, not either marketing department, not the press. Every word serves the buyer. Co-branding is structural (joint logo treatment) but not editorial (no "we are excited to partner…").
- **"Axe what's not essential."** A joint brief carries 2× the temptation: each side wants its features, its boilerplate, its press quote. Smart Brevity gives us the principle that lets us cut both. 1-2 pages, body copy 250-500 words MAX (a touch more than the solo brief because one extra paragraph belongs to integration mechanics).
- **Short, Not Shallow.** Concision through specificity, not through omission. The joint brief still names the trigger, the gap, the combined answer, the proof, the qualifying question — but each in a single tight line.
- **Tease / stakes before payoff.** Pull the buyer in with the trigger event, not with the partnership announcement.

Memorable quote applied: "Short, not shallow." A joint brief that hits 300 words and earns the next conversation beats a 1,200-word press release any day.

## 2. What the corpus gave us (top citations)

Corpus query returned 10 chunks. The synthesized response from Sherpa was unusually crisp and is the operational source for the partner-GTM-specific structural sections.

1. **Maja Voje — The Go To Market Strategist (pp. 286-287)** — Partnership framework: define mutual value, align on timeline and audience, then build enablement assets that match the sales motion. The brief is the customer-facing OUTPUT of that alignment, not a substitute for it. Adopted as a pre-work check (the alignment must already exist; the brief doesn't create it).
2. **PMA — "Leading co-marketing efforts with partners" (Considerations and tips)** — Joint customer profile must be agreed by both sides before drafting; the alignment IS the asset. Adopted into pre-work.
3. **PMA — "Leading co-marketing efforts with partners" (A strategic approach to growth)** — Sellers need a story to tell, not a slide to show. Adopted as the "better-together" proof point requirement.
4. **Martina Lauchengco — Loved (p. 78)** — Solutions marketing register: describe what the customer's world looks like AFTER, not what the products DO. Direct lift into the joint outcomes section.
5. **PMA — "What is solutions marketing" (Defining solutions marketing)** — Solutions framing for multi-product / multi-vendor offerings; the brief is a solutions-marketing artifact, not a product-marketing artifact for either side. Adopted as the voice rule.
6. **PMA — "What is solutions marketing" (Building stronger customer relationships)** — Joint customer outcomes over feature lists. Reinforces Lauchengco.
7. **PMA — "What is solutions marketing" (Conclusion)** — Three-outcome cap, measurability test ("Is this something the buyer's boss cares about?").
8. **Kiran Khanna / PMA — "Lead effective and contextual GTM" (Speak less, show more)** — Reinforces brevity discipline; the proof point does the persuasion, not the prose.
9. **PMA — "Technical writer to PMM transition" (Partner marketing and collaboration)** — Loose adjacency on partner-collateral mechanics; not dominant.

### Net-new contributions from the corpus (beyond Smart Brevity)

The corpus carried four structural sections that Smart Brevity (a generic brevity book) couldn't supply, and that distinguish a *joint* solution brief from a solo brief:

- **The Joint VP Test.** "If either partner could say the same thing solo, you don't have a joint value proposition. You have two value propositions stapled together." This is the single most important quality test for the artifact and is embedded directly in the Joint Value Proposition section description.
- **Trigger / Gap / Combined Answer triplet.** The structural shape of the joint LEDE — name the trigger event, name the gap when it goes unsolved, name the combined answer only the two vendors deliver. This is sharper than a generic Power Statement because it forces the wedge to be joint.
- **Three joint customer outcomes — measurable AND boss-cares-about-it test.** Two-part qualifier per outcome. Without this, joint outcomes drift into integration features ("real-time bi-directional sync") instead of buyer-world outcomes ("your sales team sees accurate pipeline every morning without waiting for IT").
- **Co-sell handoff language as a rep-facing block.** When Partner A is in the deal, what do they say to bring Partner B in? Ambiguity here kills co-sell momentum. The handoff is a sentence, not a process. Embedded as an explicit section so neither side has to invent it on the fly.
- **Qualifying question — exactly one.** "Do you currently have X problem?" If yes, the brief is relevant; if no, move on. One question, not four (artifact 27's four-question pattern is for the partner enablement one-pager, where the rep is qualifying the customer for OUR product; here the buyer-facing brief carries one signal-clarifier).

### Significant corpus gap (logged)

- **No partner-co-marketing asset specifications surfaced.** No AWS Partner Network solution-brief specs, Google Cloud Marketplace co-sell asset templates, Salesforce ISV partner one-pager templates, Crossbeam / Reveal joint-asset patterns, or hyperscaler partner-portal asset requirements. The 1-2 page constraint, joint-logo treatment guidance, and integration-mechanics block are extrapolated from the synthesis and from artifact-18 / artifact-27 inheritance. The synthesis is internally consistent and corroborated by Voje's GTM framework, but the corpus citations don't ground hyperscaler / marketplace specifics.
- **No SI / consulting partner co-sell collateral surfaced.** The brief shape we shipped is closest to ISV-with-ISV co-sell. SI motions (Accenture, Deloitte, regional SIs) likely need a different mechanic block. Out of scope for v1; flagged for next ingestion sweep.

### Where book and corpus disagreed — and which won

No direct conflicts. Smart Brevity supplies the structural spine (Core 4, brevity discipline, audience-first, axe-what's-not-essential). The corpus supplies the partner-GTM-specific structural sections (Joint VP Test, trigger/gap/combined answer, three-outcome rule, handoff language, single qualifying question). They compose cleanly. Where the corpus reached for brevity language ("speak less, show more"), Smart Brevity was sharper and we used Smart Brevity's framing.

### Where they merged

- **Failure-mode list.** Smart Brevity's fog-of-words / inside-out / jargon-as-trust failure modes + corpus practitioner pitfalls (joint-VP-stapled, two-logos-and-a-handshake, press-release voice, missing handoff language, missing qualifying question, feature-list joint outcomes, partner-press-quote bait) merged into 12 negative-guidance rules. The 12-rule count is higher than artifact 18's 9 because the joint asset carries strictly more failure modes than the solo brief — every solo failure mode applies, plus six joint-specific ones.
- **Skeleton order.** Smart Brevity Core 4 = TEASE-LEDE-WHY-IT-MATTERS-GO-DEEPER, mapped to: joint headline (TEASE), joint Power Statement (LEDE), three joint outcomes (WHY IT MATTERS), how-it-works + joint customer proof (GO DEEPER), then the rep-facing handoff block + single qualifying question + dual CTA. The dual CTA is a co-marketing convention (one path each side, or one shared joint path) — not in Smart Brevity.

## 3. Design decisions (template structure)

**One brief, one (vendor × partner) pairing, one shared use case.** The MCP tool generates one brief per call. If the partnership covers three use cases, that's three briefs — the trigger, the gap, and the combined answer differ in each. Multi-use-case briefs collapse into press-release voice within two paragraphs (corpus failure mode #1).

**1-2 pages enforced visually.** Inherited from Smart Brevity discipline + artifact 18 precedent. The skeleton renders sections as compact blocks with terseness instructions ("3 joint outcomes max", "ONE customer proof", "ONE qualifying question", "3 max steps in How It Works"). Body copy 250-500 words target.

**Joint logo treatment as a structural directive, not a design tip.** Header block names both companies side-by-side, equal visual weight, in the order agreed by the partnership team (alphabetical default if no agreed order). Vendor's logo first or partner's logo first is set in pre-work; doesn't change during drafting. This is encoded in the skeleton because partnership disputes about logo order kill briefs at the legal-review stage.

**Skeleton order = Core 4 mapped joint-first.**
- Pre-work (alignment check + logo / persona / motion decisions)
- Joint headline (TEASE)
- Trigger / Gap / Combined Answer (the LEDE shape)
- Joint Power Statement (LEDE confirmation)
- Three joint customer outcomes (WHY IT MATTERS)
- How It Works — 3 max steps showing integration / division of labor (GO DEEPER part 1)
- ONE joint customer proof (GO DEEPER part 2)
- Co-sell handoff language (rep-facing, internal-mode block embedded in the leave-behind)
- ONE qualifying question
- Dual CTA (one per side OR one shared joint path)
- Joint footer (both PMM owners, partnership program contacts on both sides, joint refresh date)

**Joint VP Test embedded in the section, not as a separate validation step.** "If either partner could say the same thing solo, this isn't a joint VP." Applied during drafting, not after, because once two press releases are stapled together they're hard to un-staple.

**Three joint outcomes, locked at three.** Inherited from Lauchengco + PMA solutions-marketing canon. Each outcome must pass the two-part test: measurable AND boss-cares-about-it. More than three and the brief regresses to a feature list × 2 vendors.

**ONE customer proof, BOTH products in production, joint outcome only.** Sharper than artifact 18's "one of three formats". Joint briefs are not allowed to fall back to logo clusters because the cluster is a partnership-page visual, not buyer evidence. If no joint reference yet, the slot renders explicit "TBD — first joint reference deal in motion as of [date]" rather than auto-fallback to logos.

**Co-sell handoff language as a rep-facing block in a buyer-facing asset.** Unusual decision: most leave-behinds are pure buyer-facing. We embed a "When Partner A is in the deal, here's the line that brings Partner B in" block because the brief travels in two field orgs simultaneously and the handoff is the highest-friction moment in co-sell. The block is short, in plain language, and is intentionally readable by the buyer too — the buyer seeing the handoff line builds confidence that this is a real partnership, not a marketing photo op.

**ONE qualifying question (not four).** Boundary call vs artifact 27. Artifact 27 has four qualifying questions because the partner rep is qualifying for OUR product. Artifact 29 has one because the buyer (or any rep) needs a single signal-clarifier — "Do you currently have X problem?" — that decides whether the brief is relevant.

**Dual CTA pattern.** Either two CTAs (one for each side — "Talk to [Vendor]" and "Talk to [Partner]") OR one shared joint CTA ("Book a 30-minute joint discovery call"). Decision is set in pre-work. Both sides agree before drafting; doesn't change during drafting.

**Footer is two-sided.** Two PMM owners, two partnership program contacts, joint refresh date. Single-side ownership = one re-org from abandonment.

### Trade-offs we accepted

- **No multi-use-case coverage.** That would be a partnership solutions catalog — not in scope.
- **No internal-only sections.** This is buyer-facing. The internal-only co-sell mechanics (deal-reg, comp structure, partner economics) live in artifact 28 (co-sell battlecard).
- **No solo-product positioning content.** Inherits from positioning (01) and ICP (04) on our side; assumes the partner has equivalent positioning on their side.
- **No embedded pricing for either product.** Joint pricing is rarely a one-pager artifact and pricing leaks badly. Reference current pricing pages on both sides if needed.
- **No partner program / tier badges.** The brief is buyer-facing, not partner-program marketing.
- **No partner-press-quote bait.** "We're excited to partner with…" quote at the bottom is a press-release tic, not a leave-behind feature.

## 4. Open questions for audit

- **Should the co-sell handoff block be visible to the buyer or rendered as a separate rep-facing tear-off?** Currently embedded in the buyer-facing brief because the buyer seeing the handoff line builds partnership confidence. Argument for tear-off: cleaner buyer-facing piece. Argument for current: the brief is already 1-2 pages; a tear-off would fragment.
- **Should the customer proof refuse to render if no joint reference exists yet?** Currently allows "TBD — first joint reference deal in motion as of [date]". Argument for refusing: a joint brief without joint proof is partnership-marketing, not solutions-marketing. Argument for current: PMM teams build joint briefs before the first joint reference and the explicit TBD surfaces the gap.
- **Dual CTA vs single shared CTA — should we lock to one pattern?** Currently a pre-work decision. Both work; locking would be cleaner but loses flexibility for partnerships where the two sales motions truly differ.
- **Should the qualifying question be one or two?** One per corpus. Open whether to allow "trigger question + disqualifier question" pair when the disqualifier is non-obvious.
- **Corpus gap (logged):** no hyperscaler partner-portal asset specs (AWS APN, GCP Marketplace, Azure ISV) surfaced; no SI / consulting partner collateral patterns. Structural decisions for joint-logo treatment and integration-mechanics block came from synthesis. Should ingest hyperscaler partner-asset documentation in next sweep.
