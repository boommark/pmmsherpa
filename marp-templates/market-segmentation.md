## Segmentation Framework

**Three dimensions that actually drive buying behavior**

We segment by the intersection of company size, vertical, and buying motion — not by firmographic buckets alone. Each dimension answers a different planning question.

**Dimension 1: Company Size (by sales team headcount)**
Drives deal economics, sales cycle length, and product adoption depth. A 30-rep team and a 300-rep team are fundamentally different buying processes even in the same vertical.

**Dimension 2: Vertical**
Drives pain priority. A SaaS company's top RI pain is coaching at scale; a financial services firm's top pain is compliance recording. Same product, different entry points and competitive sets.

**Dimension 3: Buying Motion**
Drives how the deal gets sourced and closed. Self-serve/PLG signals, ZoomInfo-native cross-sell, and outbound enterprise motions each require different sales plays, content, and success metrics.

**Why these three — not others:**
These are the dimensions where Chorus's win rate and deal economics diverge meaningfully in our own data. We have tested segmentation by geography, tech stack, and ARR — they add noise, not signal. These three cut cleanest.

## Segment Deep-Dive: Enterprise ZoomInfo Customers

**Segment 1 — Highest-priority, best win rate, largest deal size**

**Profile:**
500+ quota-carrying reps. Already contracted with ZoomInfo SalesOS at the platform level. IT has ZoomInfo in the approved vendor list. Finance has a single ZoomInfo renewal contact. This segment is our highest-conversion cross-sell motion.

**Buying process:**
Renewal-adjacent. The Chorus conversation starts 90 days before ZoomInfo contract renewal. The ZoomInfo AE owns the relationship; Chorus AE co-sells. Decision makers: VP Sales (outcome owner), RevOps (integration and data governance), CFO/Procurement (TCO and vendor count). Timeline: 30–60 days from first Chorus conversation to closed-won.

**Our win rate in this segment:** 68% (Q4 2025 — Q1 2026 cohort, n=47 opportunities)

**Why we win:** Zero incremental vendor relationship. Chorus demos in the ZoomInfo environment the customer already uses. TCO math is unambiguous: bundled vs. separate Gong contract is a 28–40% cost reduction with no integration work.

**Recommended messaging angle:** "You're already paying for the best B2B data in the world. Chorus makes it actionable in every sales conversation — without adding a vendor or a contract."

## Segment Deep-Dive: Mid-Market SaaS

**Segment 2 — High volume, fastest sales cycle, strongest word-of-mouth**

**Profile:**
50–200 reps. Series B through late D. High-growth SaaS or cloud company. Buyer is usually VP Sales or CRO who has personally felt the pain of inconsistent coaching and deal surprise. Tech-forward, willing to pilot quickly. Often not yet on ZoomInfo — this segment is an entry point to the full ZI platform.

**Buying process:**
Inbound-led or SDR-sourced. Champion is VP Sales; economic buyer is CRO or CFO at deal sizes above $60K. Evaluation is competitive — Gong is almost always in the mix, sometimes Salesloft Conversations. POC is standard; expect a 2–3 week trial. Sales cycle: 45–75 days.

**Our win rate in this segment:** 44% (competitive evaluations, Q4 2025 – Q1 2026, n=112)

**Why we win:** Speed to value. Chorus averages 3–4 week onboarding vs. Gong's 6–10 weeks for comparable-sized deployments. For a VP Sales trying to show wins in Q2, that lag is a dealbreaker. We also win on price at this tier — typically 15–20% below Gong's list price before ZoomInfo bundling.

**Recommended messaging angle:** "Most mid-market sales teams can't wait 8 weeks to start coaching. Chorus has reps recording, reviewing, and improving in week one."

## Segments to Watch

**Two emerging opportunities worth a structured pilot in H2 2026**

**Emerging Segment A: PLG + Sales-Assist (20–50 reps)**
Why interesting: SaaS companies with product-led growth are building sales-assist layers to convert high-intent product users to paying enterprise accounts. These teams are small but sophisticated — they need RI that connects product signals to conversation data, not just call recording. Chorus's ZoomInfo intent integration is a natural fit; no competitor has a credible story here yet.
Risks: Small deal size ($15–25K ARR) requires a low-touch, tech-touch motion we haven't built for. Onboarding support at this segment size is thin. Risk of churning fast if time-to-value isn't sub-2-weeks.
Entry point: Partner with ZoomInfo's PLG accounts team. 8 accounts identified as qualified. Run a 90-day pilot with dedicated CSM support; measure time-to-first-coaching-action as the north star metric.

**Emerging Segment B: International Enterprise (EMEA, APAC — 200+ reps)**
Why interesting: EMEA enterprise is underserved by every RI vendor. GDPR-compliant recording is a purchasing requirement, not a preference — and it's a barrier most US-centric vendors handle poorly. Chorus has GDPR-ready infrastructure; we simply haven't marketed it. ZoomInfo's EMEA customer base is a ready-made cross-sell list.
Risks: Localized support bandwidth is thin. French and German language transcription quality lags English. Legal and procurement cycles in EMEA run 30–45 days longer than US equivalents.
Entry point: Pilot with 5 existing ZoomInfo EMEA enterprise accounts. Set a 6-month window. If NPS > 40 and renewal rate > 80%, invest in localized CS and expand.

## Segment Prioritization

**Where to concentrate FY2027 sales and marketing investment**

| Segment | Market Attractiveness | Win Rate | Investment Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise ZI Customers** | High (large deals, fast cycle, captive audience) | 68% | Primary — maximize cross-sell coverage; all renewal expansions require a Chorus conversation |
| **Mid-Market SaaS** | High (large volume, strong word-of-mouth flywheel) | 44% | Primary — increase SDR capacity; invest in competitive battle cards and Gong displacement playbook |
| **Enterprise Greenfield** | Very High (largest deal size) | 31% | Secondary — resource-intensive; limit to accounts where ZoomInfo data advantage is pre-established |
| **Tech-Touch Mid-Market** | Medium (high volume, low deal size) | 52% | Watch — build self-serve trial motion before scaling; pilot in H2 2026 |
| **Regulated Verticals** | Medium-High (strong retention once closed) | 39% | Specialist — hire 1 vertical specialist; develop compliance-first content track |

**2×2 read:** Enterprise ZI Customers and Mid-Market SaaS are the clear Q1 priority — high attractiveness, defensible win rates. Enterprise Greenfield is the growth bet; resource selectively. Tech-Touch and Regulated Verticals are H2 2026 build items.
