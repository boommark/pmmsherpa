# Win/Loss Analysis: H1 2026
## Pipeline Management · 20 Buyer Interviews

<br>

**Interviews:** 11 wins · 9 losses · 20 total
**Methodology:** Structured 45-min buyer interviews, third-party facilitated
**Analyst:** Revenue Marketing / PMM Research
**Classification:** `INTERNAL` `EXEC DISTRIBUTION`

---

## Executive Summary

**Three findings that change how we go to market.**

**01 — We win on depth, lose on simplicity.**
Buyers who chose us consistently cited sophisticated pipeline visibility and forecast accuracy as decisive. Buyers who left cited setup complexity and time-to-value. The same product capability is both our clearest win and our most reliable loss reason — depending on who's driving the eval.

**Win rate (H1 2026):** 55% overall · 71% enterprise ($150K+ ACV) · 38% mid-market ($30–75K ACV)

**02 — The top win reason is also an untold story.**
Our AI-powered deal scoring ("Pipeline IQ") was mentioned as the deciding factor in 7 of 11 wins. It appears in zero of our public case studies. The most powerful thing we make is invisible in our marketing.

**Top win reason:** AI deal scoring / Pipeline IQ — cited by 64% of win respondents as a primary driver.

**03 — We lose mid-market deals to speed, not price.**
In 8 of 9 losses, time-to-value was cited before price. Competitors who won these deals (primarily Clari and HubSpot Deals) offered lighter implementations with faster first-value moments. We're priced appropriately — we're not set up to win fast.

**Top loss reason:** Implementation complexity / slow time-to-value — cited by 89% of loss respondents.

## Competitive Losses Breakdown

Which competitors won the deals we lost, how often, and what tipped it.

| Competitor | Deals Won Against Us | % of Losses | What Tipped It | Structural Vulnerability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Clari** | 4 | 44% | Cleaner demo narrative; stronger CFO credibility; faster perceived implementation | Our revenue orchestration story is less polished; no dedicated CFO buyer journey |
| **HubSpot Deals** | 3 | 33% | "Good enough" at a lower price point; native CRM = no integration risk; faster onboarding | Mid-market buyers willing to trade depth for speed; we don't have a land-and-expand entry motion |
| **Gong + Salesforce Forecasting** | 1 | 11% | RI team wanted Gong; RevOps used native Salesforce forecasting; no incremental budget for a third tool | Bundled stack plays are hard to displace without a consolidation narrative |

**Pattern:** We lose to simplicity, not to sophistication. Clari and HubSpot both offer a narrower feature set. They win because they're faster to value and easier to sell internally.

## The Decision Process

How buyers actually evaluated — not how we want them to evaluate.

**Committee size:** Avg. 4.2 stakeholders involved in final decision (range: 2–8). Largest committees were in enterprise wins.

**Evaluation timeline**

| Phase | Average Duration |
|---|---|
| Initial discovery and demo | 2–3 weeks |
| Shortlisting (3 to 2 vendors) | 1–2 weeks |
| Deep evaluation (POC / pilot) | 3–6 weeks |
| Legal, procurement, final approval | 2–4 weeks |
| **Total cycle** | **8–15 weeks** |

**Decision criteria — ranked by buyer priority**

1. Time-to-value and implementation risk (mentioned first in 65% of interviews)
2. Forecast accuracy and pipeline visibility depth
3. CRM integration quality (especially Salesforce custom objects)
4. Rep adoption / UI simplicity
5. Price and contract flexibility

**Who had final say**

- CRO / VP Sales: 11 of 20 deals (55%) — had final sign-off authority
- CFO / Finance: 6 of 20 deals (30%) — budget holder pulled rank in complex deals
- VP RevOps / Ops: 3 of 20 deals (15%) — technical champion with delegated authority

**Key finding:** In 6 of 9 losses, the CFO or Finance was involved in the evaluation but we never had a direct conversation with them. We sold to Sales leadership, who then had to re-sell internally to Finance without our help.
