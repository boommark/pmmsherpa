## Where They Win

Honest competitive risks — know these before you walk into the room.

**Gong's AI summaries are genuinely better today** <mark class="risk">RISK</mark>
Their SmartTracker and AI-generated deal briefs are more mature. Reps who've used Gong for 2+ years often feel the summary quality is superior. Chorus's AI is catching up but this is a real gap in accounts that live and die by call notes.

**Brand recognition creates default preference** <mark class="risk">RISK</mark>
"Just get Gong" is a default recommendation in Pavilion, RevGenius, and on LinkedIn. Buyers who haven't done a structured eval often have Gong anchored in their minds before we enter the conversation. We need to earn the eval.

**Gong Engage (sales engagement) blurs the stack** <mark class="risk">RISK</mark>
Gong is expanding into outbound sequencing with Gong Engage. For prospects who want to consolidate RI + engagement in one tool, Gong's story is newer but gaining traction. We don't have a direct equivalent.

**Coaching workflow depth** <mark class="risk">RISK</mark>
Gong's call coaching scorecards and manager dashboards are more configurable. For sales orgs that have built coaching programs around custom rubrics, migrating to Chorus requires process change management — a real switching cost.

## Objection Handlers

Real objections you'll hear. Tight, confident responses.

**"Gong has better AI summaries."**
"That's fair — Gong's SmartTracker is excellent, and we're direct about that. Here's the question though: what good is a better summary if it doesn't know who was on the call, what they've been researching, or how they show up in your ZoomInfo instance? Chorus surfaces intent context *alongside* the summary, so your reps are acting on richer signal, not just cleaner notes. And our roadmap closes this gap — we shipped three AI summary improvements last quarter alone."

**"Gong is the market standard. Our team already knows it."**
"'The market standard' in 2021 isn't the same story in 2026. Gong built its moat when revenue intelligence was a standalone category. That category has collapsed into the broader go-to-market stack. The question isn't which tool has the biggest brand — it's which tool makes *your stack* smarter. If you're already on ZoomInfo, you're leaving money on the table by running a separate Gong contract that doesn't talk to your intent data."

**"Their coaching tools are more mature."**
"Coaching depth is a real difference, and we won't pretend otherwise. What I'd push back on is: what's your coaching program actually built on today? In our experience, 80% of coaching teams use 3–4 scorecards. Chorus handles all of those out of the box. The 20% who need custom rubrics and manager cascade dashboards — that's Gong's wheelhouse, and it might be right for them. But let's validate which bucket you're in before assuming you need that depth."

## Killer Questions

Five discovery questions that surface Gong's structural weaknesses — without sounding like a takedown.

1. **"Walk me through how your reps prep for a call today — what data do they have before they hit 'Join'?"**
   *Exposes the intent gap. Gong has no pre-call intelligence layer. Chorus + ZoomInfo closes this natively.*

2. **"How many vendors touch your revenue intelligence and data enrichment workflows today?"**
   *Opens the consolidation conversation. If they're paying for Gong + ZoomInfo + a separate enrichment tool, the TCO math becomes obvious.*

3. **"When a deal goes dark, how does your team figure out why — and how long does that typically take?"**
   *Surfaces deal inspection lag. Chorus's ZoomInfo-linked contacts often surface stakeholder movement Gong can't see.*

4. **"How are your managers using call data in their 1:1s today? Is it pulling from the tool, or are reps summarizing manually?"**
   *Reveals whether coaching adoption is real or aspirational. Many Gong customers pay for coaching features they don't actively use.*

5. **"If you had to sunset one tool in your current GTM stack to hit your efficiency targets, what would it be?"**
   *Identifies consolidation pressure without asking directly. Connects back to the CFO/CRO mandate story.*
