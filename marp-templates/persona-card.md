## VP of Revenue Operations · Mid-Market SaaS · 500–2,500 employees

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Company size** | 500–2,500 employees |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS, FinTech, HRTech |
| **Team size** | 4–12 direct reports (analysts, ops managers, enablement) |
| **Reports to** | CRO or COO (occasionally CFO in finance-led orgs) |

<hr>

## A Day in the Life

- **8:00 AM:** Pulls the morning pipeline snapshot. Flags three at-risk deals before the CRO's Slack message arrives asking about the same three deals.
- **9:30 AM:** Joins the weekly forecast call. Spends 45 minutes reconciling why the Salesforce rollup disagrees with what the regional VPs submitted in the Google Sheet.
- **11:00 AM:** Unblocks a RevOps analyst who can't figure out why 38 leads weren't routed correctly from HubSpot. Traces it to a routing rule that a BDR manager quietly changed two weeks ago.
- **2:00 PM:** Reviews a new tool pitch from a vendor promising "AI-powered pipeline hygiene." Adds it to the "evaluate in Q3" backlog.
- **4:00 PM:** Builds a slide for the board deck. Manually exports, cleans, and formats data that should already be in a dashboard but isn't.

<hr>

## Goals

1. **Forecast accuracy within ±8% by end of year** — the CRO promised the board predictability; RevOps owns whether that promise holds. Misses cost credibility.
2. **Reduce time-to-close by 15%** — not by pressuring reps, but by removing friction: faster legal, cleaner handoffs, tighter deal desk SLAs.
3. **Consolidate the GTM tech stack from 14 tools to 9 without losing capability** — CFO has mandated a 20% reduction in software spend; RevOps is expected to find it.

<hr>

## Buying Triggers

1. **Missed quarter** — When a revenue miss surfaces and leadership asks "why didn't we see this coming," RevOps becomes the buyer on any tool that improves forecast visibility.
2. **New CRO or CFO hire** — Incoming execs often bring a vendor philosophy; they also open the door for RevOps to re-pitch tools the previous leadership had blocked.
3. **Tech stack audit / CFO consolidation mandate** — When software spend gets scrutinized, RevOps gets to reshape the stack. Consolidation mandates are buying opportunities, not blockers.
4. **Series C / IPO prep** — Revenue predictability becomes a board-level KPI. Every RevOps leader gets budget to fix reporting before investor scrutiny intensifies.

</div>

<div>

## Pain Points

1. **Forecast calls are a trust exercise, not a data exercise** — *Costs them:* 3–5 hours per week preparing and reconciling numbers that reps, managers, and finance all disagree on. Every missed forecast erodes their credibility with the CRO.

2. **CRM data quality is permanently broken** — *Costs them:* RevOps spends 30–40% of analyst capacity on data cleanup instead of insight generation. The root cause (rep non-compliance) is structural, not fixable through training alone.

3. **The tech stack is a 14-tool Frankenstein** — *Costs them:* Integration maintenance, duplicate data, reporting inconsistencies, and a quarterly audit where something is always broken. They own the problem even when they didn't choose the tools.

4. **No single source of truth across Sales, Marketing, and Finance** — *Costs them:* Every QBR starts with 20 minutes of "which number is right." Leadership loses trust in the data, which means they lose trust in RevOps.

5. **Deals go dark and RevOps is the last to know** — *Costs them:* Late-stage deal slippage that should have surfaced at 60% probability doesn't show up until it's at 20% — when it's too late to intervene. They own the forecast miss regardless.

<hr>

## Objections to Us

**"We just finished a 6-month Salesforce implementation. I'm not adding another layer right now."**
Translation: "I'm exhausted and politically exposed from the last project." They need to see a light integration story, not a rip-and-replace.

**"My team doesn't have bandwidth to manage another vendor relationship."**
Translation: "I'm worried about ongoing maintenance, not the initial setup." They need proof that the tool runs itself after go-live — not a dedicated admin.

**"Our reps won't adopt another tool."**
Translation: "I've been burned before." They need to hear that reps don't have to do anything differently — the data capture is automatic.

<hr>

## What Wins Them Over

**1. Show them a live forecast from their own data in the demo.**
Connect to their Salesforce sandbox and show their actual pipeline with risk scoring applied. The moment they see their deals flagged with real signals, the conversation shifts from "is this real" to "how do we get this for Q3."

**2. The CFO story closes the deal.**
Come prepared with a TCO comparison that shows integration maintenance, analyst time saved, and forecast miss reduction value. When RevOps can walk into the CFO's office with a cost-reduction story, the deal moves.

**3. Peer proof from a company their size in their vertical.**
A 20-minute reference call with a VP RevOps at a similar SaaS company is worth more than 10 slides of ROI data. Offer it early, not as a late-stage concession.
