## Category Definition

**What this market does**

Revenue Intelligence and Sales Execution platforms capture, analyze, and act on every buyer interaction — calls, emails, meetings, pipeline activity — to give sales organizations a continuous feedback loop between activity data and revenue outcomes.

**Who buys it**

- `PRIMARY BUYER` VP of Sales / CRO — owns win rate and forecast accuracy
- `SECONDARY BUYER` VP Revenue Operations — owns tooling, data integrity, and process efficiency
- `EVALUATOR` Sales Enablement Lead — owns ramp time, coaching cadence, and rep performance

**Market size signal**

$7.8B TAM (2025), growing at 18% CAGR. Consolidation accelerating: Gong, Clari, and Salesloft have each made 2+ acquisitions since 2023. Buyers increasingly evaluating platform suites over point solutions.

**How we define the category**

Platforms that provide at minimum: (1) conversation intelligence, (2) deal/pipeline inspection, and (3) coaching or engagement automation — with AI as the core differentiator, not a bolt-on.

## Where We're Vulnerable

Three honest gaps. Know these before the eval — surprises in the room are worse.

**Gap 1: AI Call Summaries and SmartTracker**

Gong's AI-generated call summaries, deal briefs, and SmartTracker are more mature. Reps who've lived in Gong for 2+ years often feel the summary quality is meaningfully better — because it is, today.

*Who exploits it:* Gong in any evaluation where the VP Sales or a champion has Gong experience. They will demo SmartTracker and ask us to match it in real time.

*Our response/roadmap:* Three AI summary improvements shipped in Q1 2026. Auto-chapter detection and deal brief generation in Q3 roadmap. Reframe: "Better summary + zero context" vs. "Good summary + full ZoomInfo context" — the integrated signal wins at the workflow level even if the summary prose loses on aesthetics.

**Gap 2: Coaching Workflow Depth**

Gong's call coaching scorecards and manager cascade dashboards are more configurable. Orgs that have built formal coaching programs around custom rubrics face real process change management when switching.

*Who exploits it:* Salesloft and Gong when the champion is a Sales Enablement leader with a mature coaching program already built.

*Our response/roadmap:* Custom scorecard builder in Q4 2026. Short-term: 80% of coaching use cases are covered out of the box; position our simplicity as a feature for orgs that haven't yet built coaching infrastructure.

**Gap 3: Brand Awareness and Analyst Coverage**

"Just get Gong" is the default recommendation in Pavilion, RevGenius, and across LinkedIn. We don't have the same organic brand pull in the RI category — customers who haven't done a structured eval often anchor to Gong before we enter the conversation.

*Who exploits it:* Gong in top-of-funnel and PLG motions. Prospects who start their eval by Googling "best revenue intelligence software" find Gong first.

*Our response/roadmap:* Targeted analyst briefings with Forrester and G2 to improve category rank. PMM to build Pavilion community playbook by Q3. Sales to deploy "earn the eval" email sequence when Gong-anchored prospects enter pipeline.

## Buyer Signals

**How to identify which competitor a prospect is currently evaluating.**

**Signals the prospect is evaluating Gong**
- Mentions "SmartTracker," "deal boards," or "Gong Engage" by name
- LinkedIn/Pavilion active — they've seen Gong content in their feed
- Already using Salesforce + no ZoomInfo contract (Gong's natural habitat)
- "We want the best AI" framing without specifying what that means
- Asks for head-to-head AI summary comparison in the first meeting

**Signals the prospect is evaluating Clari**
- CFO or FP&A is involved in the evaluation early
- Mentions "forecast accuracy," "revenue leak," or "board reporting"
- Current process: manual spreadsheet-based forecasting or Salesforce native only
- Deal size typically $200K+ ARR; complex multi-product sales org
- RevOps is the champion, not the VP Sales

**Signals the prospect is evaluating Salesloft / Outreach**
- Started conversation with "we need to fix our sequencing" — engagement is the pain, not intelligence
- VP Sales Ops or Sales Enablement leading the buy (not CRO)
- Current state: Outreach or SalesLoft legacy, looking to expand not replace
- Mentions "cadence," "dialer," or "pipeline activity" as primary language
- Mid-market, high AE headcount, rep adoption is the main concern

**Signals the prospect is running a broad platform evaluation**
- RFP in play with 5+ vendors on the list
- Procurement is leading, not Sales leadership
- Timeline is 6+ months with a "decision committee" mentioned
- Price sensitivity is explicit: asking about per-seat pricing and contract flexibility early

## Battlecard Quick-Ref

Rapid-fire positioning for the three most common head-to-head scenarios.

**vs. Gong**
Gong is the best standalone call intelligence tool money can buy — if you want to pay for a standalone tool. If you're on ZoomInfo, Chorus connects pre-call intent to post-call analysis in a single system. Better AI summaries mean nothing if they're missing the buyer context that closes deals. Our onboarding is 3x faster and our TCO for ZI customers is 28–40% lower.

**vs. Clari**
Clari is exceptional at board-facing forecast analytics — it's not a rep-facing revenue intelligence tool. If your CRO needs a forecast model, Clari wins. If your reps need to know why deals are stalling and what to do about it *in the moment*, Chorus + ZoomInfo surfaces that signal where Gong and Clari cannot. Different buyer, different job to be done.

**vs. Salesloft / Outreach**
Salesloft and Outreach built their core around sequencing and engagement — RI was bolted on. Chorus built RI first and connects it to the richest B2B database in the market. If the primary pain is "our reps aren't having enough conversations," Salesloft may be the right tool. If the pain is "our reps are having conversations but deals aren't moving," that's our category — and our data advantage is structurally unmatched.
