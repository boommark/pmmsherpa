## Audience

**Primary ICP**
- **Role:** Director of Analytics / Head of BI, or Revenue Operations Manager
- **Company:** 200–1,000 employees, Series B–D SaaS or tech-enabled services
- **Stack signal:** Looker or Tableau + Confluence + Slack — three tools to do what Helix does in one
- **Trigger:** New team hire that broke the solo-analyst model; CFO asking for self-serve dashboards; recent data team reorganization

**Secondary Audience**
- Finance Directors and VP Finance at same company profile — they receive shared reports and want commenting without needing a BI license
- IT / Data Engineering leads who manage the Looker license and are frustrated by per-seat cost scaling

<hr>

## Launch Tier

`TIER 1` — Full company-wide launch with coordinated PR, paid, and sales motion.

**Rationale:** Collaborative Workspaces is the product's primary differentiator for the next 2 quarters. Competitive window is real — Looker's collaborative features are 18 months from parity based on their public roadmap. Tier 1 justifies the $320K launch budget and dedicated SE support.

## Risks & Mitigations

**Risk 1 — Looker accelerates roadmap in response**
*Mitigation:* First-mover content locked in before launch. Customer case studies published within 14 days of GA. Analyst briefings scheduled T-2 weeks so Gartner commentary leads any competitive response.

**Risk 2 — Sales team not demo-ready at launch**
*Mitigation:* SE-led live demo rehearsal May 20 is mandatory. Recorded walkthrough available in Highspot by May 17 for async prep. AEs without completed certification don't get Tier 1 lead routing.

**Risk 3 — In-product adoption lags (feature is buried)**
*Mitigation:* UX tooltip tour ships with launch. If 30-day adoption <12%, trigger a targeted in-app prompt campaign to the bottom-50% engagement cohort. Priya M. owns the go/no-go call at Day 14.
