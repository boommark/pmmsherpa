## Objection Messaging

**"We already have Salesforce. Why do we need another layer?"**
Salesforce is a system of record — it only knows what reps tell it. Clari is a system of intelligence — it captures what actually happens: every email, meeting, and stakeholder engagement, automatically. Together they're a complete picture. Separately, Salesforce is an expensive spreadsheet with a CRM wrapper.

**"Our RevOps team just finished a Salesforce customization. We don't want to break what we built."**
Clari doesn't replace your Salesforce configuration — it reads from it and writes back to it. Your custom fields, validation rules, and stage logic stay exactly as built. Implementation is typically 3–4 weeks with zero disruption to existing workflows.

**"We can't get our reps to log activity reliably. Why would adding another tool fix that?"**
It won't — and that's exactly the point. Clari doesn't ask reps to log anything. It captures email, calendar, call, and engagement data automatically. Rep compliance stops being a RevOps problem because rep input is no longer required.

## Do / Don't

| Say This | Not That |
|---|---|
| "Revenue operations platform" | "Sales forecasting tool" — undersells platform scope, positions against lightweight point solutions |
| "AI-driven revenue signal capture" | "AI-powered CRM" — implies replacement; buyers add $500K in switching costs mentally |
| "One source of truth for every revenue team" | "Single pane of glass" — overused, implies dashboard, not workflow transformation |
| "Forecast confidence" | "Forecast accuracy" — accuracy implies prediction; confidence implies better decision-making inputs |
| "Your CFO will trust the number" | "Reduces spreadsheets" — too tactical; CFO buyers need board-level value, not efficiency claims |
| "Captures every revenue signal automatically" | "Improves CRM hygiene" — hygiene is a RevOps-only concern; signal capture resonates with every persona |
