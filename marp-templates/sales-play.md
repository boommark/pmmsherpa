## The Buyer

**Title:** VP of Revenue Operations (or Sr. Director RevOps)

Series C–D SaaS, 200–1,000 employees, $50M–$500M ARR. Salesforce as system of record. Using 8–15 point tools in the GTM stack. Under board pressure to improve net revenue retention and cut burn.

**Strategic priorities right now:**

- Increase pipeline visibility without adding headcount

## The Trigger Moments

Four events that signal this buyer is ready to move — right now.

**1. New CRO hire (0–90 days in)**
New CRO is auditing every tool in the stack. They want wins fast. This is the single best entry point — RevOps is scrambling to justify the budget *before* the CRO forms opinions.

**2. Missed quota two quarters running**
When attainment drops below 75% for two consecutive quarters, the board demands a pipeline quality audit. RevOps owns the answer. They need better deal inspection — yesterday.

**3. Active vendor consolidation mandate**
CFO has told the CRO to cut SaaS spend by 20%. RevOps is handed the list. Any tool that can replace two tools wins the conversation automatically.

**4. Failed Salesforce integration (another tool)**
A broken sync with their CRM — from any tool — is a moment of high frustration and openness to alternatives. Ask: "What's your current data flow from call recordings to Salesforce?"

**5. RevOps headcount freeze**
No new hires approved for the next two quarters. Every manual workflow is now a liability. Automation and consolidation become survival, not strategy.

## Common Objections

Four objections you will hear. Two-column layout — know your responses cold.

**"We just renewed our existing tool 8 months ago."**
Renewal timing is not a reason to stay with a broken process. The question is: what does the last 8 months of data tell you about forecast accuracy and rep ramp? If those numbers are where you want them, great. If not, the renewal is a sunk cost — not a reason to wait another 16 months. We can time a migration to land at your natural renewal.

**"Our Salesforce admin would need to approve this."**
Bring them in. We have a pre-built Salesforce managed package that deploys in 90 minutes. Your admin will spend 2 hours in setup versus months maintaining a custom API integration. We'd love to do a 20-minute technical deep-dive with them.

**"We don't have budget right now."**
Tell me what you're currently paying for your call recording tool, your data enrichment tool, and your forecast layer separately. In 80% of our deals, consolidating those three into one contract saves $40–80K per year. This is usually budget-neutral or net positive in year one.

**"We tried a similar tool 2 years ago and it didn't stick."**
What killed it? Adoption is the single most common failure mode, not technology. We have a dedicated RevOps enablement playbook — not a generic onboarding deck — built specifically for teams coming off failed implementations. Three of our best case studies started as failure recoveries from competitors.

## Discovery Questions

Six questions in three categories. Use Situation to open, Problem to deepen, Implication to close the discovery.

**Situation (open the conversation)**

1. *"Walk me through how your forecast gets built today — what data sources flow into that number, and how much of it is automated versus manual?"*
   — Maps the current integration pain and surfaces manual reconciliation time.

2. *"How many tools currently touch your revenue intelligence workflow — from first call to closed-won in Salesforce?"*
   — Surfaces the consolidation opportunity. Most RevOps leads undercount until they list them out loud.

**Problem (go deeper)**

3. *"When a deal slips or goes dark, how does your team typically find out — and how long does it take from when it happens to when RevOps knows?"*
   — Quantifies deal inspection lag. The answer is almost always "too long."

4. *"What does your CRO do in the 24 hours before a board forecast call? Is the data ready, or is RevOps building it?"*
   — This question lands differently with RevOps leaders. It surfaces the trust gap between RevOps output and CRO confidence.

**Implication (connect pain to cost)**

5. *"If your average rep is taking 4–5 months to ramp, what's the dollar value of compressing that by 6 weeks — and what would it take to make that happen?"*
   — Forces the buyer to do the math themselves. A number they generate is more credible than one you give them.

6. *"If your next board forecast is off by more than 15%, what happens? What's the conversation your CRO has to have?"*
   — This one creates urgency without manufactured pressure. Let the answer do the work.

- Prospect has **custom Salesforce objects** or a RevOps data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery)

- Buyer is asking about **data residency, SOC 2 scope, or SSO configuration**
- **Technical RevOps champion** (vs. business buyer) wants to see API docs or data lineage
- Prospect mentions a **competing evaluation** already in progress — escalate immediately
