Positioning that doesn't sound like every other SaaS company

Hi,

Positioning is the hardest thing in product marketing. Most teams end up with some version of "the leading platform for [category]" because they tried one framework, filled in the blanks, and called it done. The result sounds professional but says nothing specific.

Good positioning requires thinking across at least four dimensions: who you're competing against (Dunford), whether you're creating or joining a category (Lochhead), where you sit on the adoption curve (Moore), and what language your competitors have already claimed (Ries & Trout). PMM Sherpa works across all four simultaneously. It flags the tensions between them and gives you a positioning statement that holds up under scrutiny.

POSITIONING STATEMENT

"Write a positioning statement for [product]. We compete mainly against [competitors]. Our ICP is [describe them]. Use Dunford's framework as the base, but cross-check against Lochhead's category design and Moore's adoption curve. No generic SaaS language."

Tip: Name your actual competitors. Sherpa can pull their current positioning from their live sites and find the gaps you can own.

PRESSURE TEST

"Here's our current positioning: [paste it]. Pressure-test it. Where is it vague? Where would a buyer not understand how we're different? Rewrite the weak parts and explain what you changed and why."

Tip: Include your current homepage headline, tagline, and boilerplate. Sherpa will tell you which parts are working and which are filler.

MESSAGING HIERARCHY

"Build a messaging hierarchy for [product]. Top-level value prop, 3 supporting pillars, and proof points for each. Our audience is [describe them] and the main alternative they use today is [current solution]."

Tip: Mention the alternative they're using today (even if it's spreadsheets or manual processes). Positioning against "the status quo" is often more important than positioning against a named competitor.
