This is just a quick note to let you know I’ve got **[a guest post over on Lenny’s newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/a-guide-to-advanced-b2b-positioning)** today. For this one, I’ve decided to do a deep dive into some of the trickier positioning roadblocks I’ve seen and how teams can get around them. This is a very long, crunchy post. I will be answering questions over there today, so please head to Lenny’s newsletter and let me know what you think!

[![Image 4](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IzLa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdecce787-b15f-49ca-9f44-a1eccff9fa4e_1456x970.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IzLa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdecce787-b15f-49ca-9f44-a1eccff9fa4e_1456x970.png)

I rarely do guest posts - this one was fun to do and represents a different lens on a set of topics I cover in the new book.

* * *

I’m putting the finishing touches on a new set of templates for the updated version of [Obviously Awesome](https://www.aprildunford.com/books). By popular demand, I’m including a set of completed templates and an example sales pitch. Are there other things you felt were missing from the templates? Let me know!
